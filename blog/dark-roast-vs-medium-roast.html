Dark Roast vs Medium Roast: Flavour, Body & Caffeine Myths Debunked

Roast level changes how heat transforms sugars and acids inside the bean. It is not a simple "stronger caffeine" dial — extraction, dose, and grind size usually move the needle more than roast colour alone.

This guide breaks down the science behind dark roast vs medium roast coffee, debunks common caffeine myths, compares flavor profiles and body, and helps you choose the right Coffee+ blend for your taste, routine, and brewing setup — all using 100% ethically sourced Ethiopian Arabica.

The Roasting Process: What Actually Changes

Coffee roasting is a controlled application of heat that triggers chemical reactions inside the green bean. As temperature rises:

  • First Crack (~205°C): Beans expand, moisture escapes, light roast stage begins
  • Development Phase: Sugars caramelize, acids break down, oils migrate to surface
  • Second Crack (~225°C): Oils emerge, body intensifies, dark roast stage begins

The longer the roast, the more origin flavors (fruit, florals, tea-like notes) give way to roast-derived flavors (chocolate, smoke, caramel). But caffeine? It remains remarkably stable across roast levels.

Myth Busted: Does Dark Roast Have More Caffeine?

Short answer: No. Caffeine is thermally stable and doesn't significantly degrade during roasting. The perceived "strength" of dark roast comes from:

  • Flavor density: Bold, smoky notes feel more intense
  • Lower acidity: Smoother mouthfeel reads as "stronger"
  • Psychological association: Darker color = stronger in many cultures

What actually affects caffeine content more than roast level:

Factor Impact on Caffeine Practical Tip
Brew Method Espresso > Drip > French Press (concentration) Choose method based on desired intensity, not roast
Coffee-to-Water Ratio More coffee = more caffeine per cup Use a scale for consistency (1:16 starting point)
Grind Size Finer grind = faster extraction = more caffeine Match grind to brew method; don't over-extract
Bean Origin Robusta has ~2x caffeine vs Arabica Coffee+ uses 100% Arabica for smooth, focused energy

Dark Roast: Flavor Profile & Best Use Cases

Darker roasts emphasise roast-derived flavors developed during extended heat exposure:

  • Flavor notes: Dark chocolate, toasted caramel, subtle smoke, roasted nuts
  • Body: Heavy, syrupy, coating mouthfeel
  • Acidity: Low — smooth, gentle on sensitive stomachs
  • Aroma: Rich, deep, comforting

Best for:

  • Late-night coding or study sessions (when you accept the caffeine trade-off)
  • Milk-based drinks (cappuccino, latte) — roast flavors cut through dairy
  • Cold brew — low acidity prevents harshness when steeped long
  • Drinkers who prefer bold, "classic coffee" taste

Medium Roast: Flavor Profile & Best Use Cases

Medium roasts preserve more of the bean's origin character — the unique flavors imparted by Ethiopian terroir, altitude, and processing:

  • Flavor notes: Citrus, berry, floral jasmine, honey, tea-like complexity
  • Body: Medium — balanced, versatile mouthfeel
  • Acidity: Medium — bright but not sharp; adds liveliness
  • Aroma: Fragrant, nuanced, inviting

Best for:

  • Morning focus blocks — balanced caffeine without overstimulation
  • Black coffee drinkers who appreciate complexity
  • Pour-over or Aeropress brewing — highlights origin notes
  • All-day sipping — approachable flavor that doesn't fatigue the palate

Coffee+ offers two medium-range options:

  • Nostalgia (true medium): Balanced, comforting, origin-forward — ideal for relaxed mornings or afternoon reflection
  • Energizer (medium-dark): Pushes slightly darker for more momentum — perfect for study sprints or focused work blocks

Brewing Tips by Roast Level

Roast level affects extraction — adjust your technique accordingly:

  • Dark roast (Extravaganza): Extracts faster. Use slightly coarser grind or shorten brew time by 15–30 seconds to avoid bitterness.
  • Medium roast (Nostalgia/Energizer): More forgiving. Standard ratios (1:16 coffee-to-water) and brew times work well.
  • Water temperature: Dark roasts tolerate slightly cooler water (90–93°C); medium roasts shine at 93–96°C.
  • Grind consistency: Use a burr grinder for even extraction — critical for highlighting origin notes in medium roasts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does dark roast have more caffeine than medium roast?

No — caffeine content is very similar across roast levels. The difference is flavor density, acidity, and body. Dark roasts taste bolder and smoother; medium roasts preserve more origin character and brightness. What affects caffeine more: brew method, coffee-to-water ratio, and grind size.

Which roast is better for sensitive stomachs?

Dark roasts are typically lower in acidity and may be gentler on sensitive stomachs. Coffee+ Extravaganza (dark roast) is formulated for smooth, low-acid enjoyment during long sessions. If you're very sensitive, try pairing coffee with food and staying hydrated.

How do I choose between Extravaganza, Energizer, and Nostalgia?

Extravaganza (dark): bold, low-acid for late nights. Energizer (medium-dark): balanced, motivating for focus blocks. Nostalgia (medium): comforting, origin-forward for all-day sipping. Order a sample pack to test all three before committing to a full bag.

Does roast level affect brewing method?

Yes. Dark roasts extract faster — use slightly coarser grind or shorter brew time to avoid bitterness. Medium roasts are more forgiving with standard ratios. We include roast-specific brewing tips with every Coffee+ order, and our blog has step-by-step guides.
